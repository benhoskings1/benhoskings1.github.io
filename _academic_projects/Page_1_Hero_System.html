Project Description

Abstract

The proposed Health and Emotion Remote Observation (HERO) system successfully evaluates symptoms of neurodegenerative diseases (NDs) across three core components: physical, cognitive, and wellbeing assessments. This is achieved via a research-backed digital consultation experience that employs the use of questions, cognitive tests, and sensor-based evaluations to collect a holistic data sample of neurodegenerative disease (ND) symptoms in the user. Users complete this consultation by interacting with an intuitive and accessible device that has centered user feedback in the iteration of its design. These interactions provide a multi-modal evaluation of ND progression in the user which is communicated to healthcare professionals (HCP)s via a practical dashboard. Ultimately, the HERO System acts as a successful prototype for missing components in long-term ND care and helps to alleviate the challenges associated with long-term ND prognosis for both patients and practitioners.
